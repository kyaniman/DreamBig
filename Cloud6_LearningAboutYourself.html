Cloud 6 - Leaning About Yourself: Knowing what type of learner you are, and discovering your strengths and opportunities.

Learn About Yourself

The VARK Questionnaire

In order to know what type of learner you are, you need to know what type of learner you are. Before you take the VARK Questionnaire, try to guess what type of learner you are. It may be that you think that you are one type of learner, but the questionnaire reveals that you are another type. If you feel that you are another type of learner, please try to consider what the questionnaire reveals to you. Try to be open-minded. There may be another way you enjoy learning about things!


Click the link below to open the VARK Questionnaire:


http://vark-learn.com/the-vark-questionnaire/


Study habits can be improved if you know the type of learner you are. Here are some useful tips to consider:


Aural/Auditory Learners: If you prefer learning by listening for information, consider buying audiobooks. You can listen to the material instead of reading it. Luckily, there are so many audiobooks out there that students can buy. I recommend using Amazon to search for those audiobooks. If you are an audio learner, try to listen to the material several times. This will aid in remember it for the future.


Read/Write Learners: If you prefer learning by reading/writing material down, I suggest investing in sticky notes, flashcards, notepads, and notebooks. So, once you read the material, you'll be able to immediately write it down on these to remember it for the future.


Kinesthetic Learners: If you prefer doing things by hand and practicing them, then see what you can do to get hands-on practice regarding what you've learned. Sometimes you may even consider looking for videos online that simulate what you're trying to learn. This will give you an idea of what to do when the real-life situation occurs. 


Visual Learners: If you prefer looking at visual representation of the material, then this type of learning is for you. This can be visual representation of data, graphs, and charts. Try to research and apply this to assignments when you can. For example, if you encounter an assignment that gives you a fact, try to look up a visual representation of that fact to get a better understanding of the material.


Multimodality: Basically, this is for people who do not favor just one type of learning. They want to try everything to explore the ins-and-outs of the material they would like to learn. If you think this is you, do as much research on the topic you're trying to learn. Also, try to network with professionals of that field. They may give  you an opportunitiy to get hands-on experience with the subject!
